Panda

The giant panda, native to the bamboo forests of China, is one of the most beloved animals in the world. Known for its distinctive black and white fur, the panda has a playful yet gentle nature. Despite being classified as a carnivore, pandas primarily eat bamboo, consuming up to 40 pounds a day! With their strong jaws and teeth, they easily break through tough bamboo stalks. Giant pandas are solitary animals, spending most of their time eating and resting. Conservation efforts have helped increase their population, though they remain a vulnerable species. Pandas symbolize peace and harmony in Chinese culture, and seeing one is always a delight for zoo visitors!
