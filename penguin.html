Penguin

Penguins are flightless birds that thrive in some of the world's coldest environments, with most species living in the Southern Hemisphere. Their sleek, black and white bodies are perfectly adapted for swimming, using their flipper-like wings to glide gracefully through the water. Penguins are expert hunters, feeding primarily on fish, squid, and krill. They can dive to great depths and hold their breath for several minutes while searching for food. On land, penguins have a charming, waddling walk and are known for their strong social bonds, often huddling together to keep warm in cold climates. Penguins are a symbol of resilience and adaptability, delighting visitors with their playful behavior and unique appearance.
